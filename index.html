Cost of migration versus awareness

For those who are aware of the migration cost beforehand,

Cost of Knowledge: Single mother households spend more on intemediaries





Single mother households in Central America face more obstacles than biparental households.


  • Women are less likely to own their home or own a bank account
  • Single mother households require more preparation to migrate
  • Single mother households have a cost of knowledge. They are more likely to be unaware of the costs of migration, and as a result have a higher cost of migration
  • Specifically, single mother households spend a higher percentage of their remittances on intermediaries.
